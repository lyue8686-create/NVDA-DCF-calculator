NASDAQ: NVDA  ·  S&P 500  ·  Philadelphia Semiconductor Index

NVIDIA designs and manufactures graphics processing units (GPUs) and system-on-chip units. Originally known for PC gaming graphics cards, NVIDIA has become the dominant supplier of AI accelerators used to train and run large language models, data centre workloads, and autonomous vehicles. Its CUDA software ecosystem, built over two decades, creates a deep competitive moat that rivals have struggled to replicate. The Blackwell GPU architecture (launched FY26) drives the current hypergrowth cycle.

Key Competitive Advantages
CUDA Ecosystem
18M+ developers, 3,500+ GPU-accelerated applications. Decade-long head start vs. AMD ROCm and Intel oneAPI.
NVLink / NVSwitch
GPU-to-GPU interconnect enabling 576-GPU Blackwell clusters. Up to 1.8TB/s bandwidth — no rival offers comparable scale.
Full-Stack AI Platform
Hardware + CUDA + cuDNN + TensorRT + Triton + NIM microservices. Customers buy a platform, not a chip.
Supply Chain Control
Deep TSMC 4N/3N allocation, CoWoS-L HBM packaging partnerships, and long-term supply agreements through FY27+.
Key Risk Factors
Export Controls
US BIS restrictions on H100/H200/B200 to China. ~17% of FY26 revenue at risk. A800/H800 workarounds also restricted.
Customer Concentration
Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta represent >40% of Data Centre revenue. Any capex pullback materially impacts results.
Competition
AMD MI300X gaining traction. Custom silicon (TPU v5, Trainium2, Maia 100) from hyperscalers. Qualcomm/Intel targeting edge AI.
Supply Constraints
CoWoS advanced packaging bottleneck at TSMC. HBM3e supply limited by SK Hynix/Samsung/Micron capacity. Demand exceeds supply through FY27.
Valuation Risk
Trading at >30× EV/Sales and ~50× forward earnings. Priced for perfection — any miss or guidance cut risks sharp de-rating.
AI Spend Cycle
Hyperscaler AI capex could plateau or rotate to inference chips (lower ASP). Revenue heavily dependent on continued training-cluster build-out.
